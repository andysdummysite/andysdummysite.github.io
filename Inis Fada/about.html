Our History

Band History

The Inis Fada Gaelic Pipe Band is one of the oldest and best pipe bands on Long Island. We have been sponsored by the Irish American Society of Nassau, Suffolk, and Queens for over 50 years. The Band was originally formed by Pipe Major Emeritus James Hagan (deceased), Pipe Sergeant James O'Donnell (deceased), Drum Sergeant James Nilan (deceased), and Band Secretary Tom Breen (deceased), in 1959. We made our debut as the lead band in the Hempstead (now Mineola) St. Patrick's Day Parade on March 12, 1961. Believe it or not, one of the original members, Mike Conolly Sr. (father of the current Pipe Major), is still active in the band today.

Through the Years

Over the years the Band has distinguished itself in many different arenas. Inis Fada Gaelic has achieved Northeastern United States Champion status in Grade IV, Grade III and Grade II competition. We have entertained at many gala events in New York City, and in Washington DC. Most memorable were the Embassy Balls at the Irish Embassy in Washington D.C., and Presidential fund raisers at the National Armory and more recently our trip to Glasgow Scotland to compete in the bagpipe band World Championships.

Although all but one of the old timers have retired, the tradition of top level competition playing is maintained by the second and third generation of pipers and drummers. The sons and daughters of the original band members, along with a healthy infusion of experienced newcomers, have taken over the Band. The current Band Officers are Ward Conolly, Pipe Major, Bill Bochanowicz, Pipe Sergeant and Mike Gray, Drum Sergeant. The Drum Major's position, which is largely ceremonial, is maintained by "old timer" Mike Conolly Sr.
